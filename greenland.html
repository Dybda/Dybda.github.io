Shopping for Sovereignty

Describing Trump's Influence on Political Communication in Denmark and Greenland

Status: Registered Report (March 2025) Methods: Embedding Regression / Frame Analysis

With Piotr Marczynski (Université Libre de Bruxelles)

Abstract

Political communication dynamics are increasingly shaped by external interventions from state actors, yet rarely has it happened and rarely has it been researched how such interventions affect discourses in post-colonial relationships within a commonwealth. This study investigates how Trump's 2024 reiterated proposition to purchase Greenland reconfigured the discursive relationship between Danish and Greenlandic political elites leading up to Greenland's election.

Employing embedding regression techniques, we will analyze Facebook communications from all elected officials in both parliaments to quantify changes in frame adoption and propagation patterns. We will describe how Trump's intervention intensified independence-oriented discourse among Greenlandic politicians and how it affected the commonwealth framing among Danish counterparts. Our results will advance the theoretical understanding of how external interventions affect post-colonial communication dynamics.

Research Questions

  • How does Trump's reframing of the topic of the status of Greenland impact the entrenched debate between Danish and Greenlandic political elites?
  • How might Greenlandic independence party members respond?
  • How might Danish governing coalition respond to a threat to the kingdom?
  • Would Greenlandic politicians feel closer to Denmark or would they view this discourse as a repetition of existing sentiments of not being recognized as autonomous and self-governing?

Theoretical Framework

We apply cascading activation framework (Entman, 2003; Entman & Usher, 2018) adapted for cross-national contexts. Trump functions as an exogenous "rogue actor" who utilizes the digital environment to inject novel frames into established discourse patterns. The analysis draws on McLuhan's media ecology theory and Deutsch's cybernetic model of political systems to understand how the medium and form of Trump's communication created immediate diplomatic disruption.

We conceptualize discursive power following Jungherr et al. (2019) as actors' ability to introduce, amplify and maintain topics and frames. This framework allows examination of how power asymmetries between post-colonial actors condition their differential capacity to contest or reinforce externally introduced frames.

Data & Methods

  • Facebook communications from all elected Danish (Folketinget: 179) and Greenlandic (Inatsisartut: 31) politicians
  • Time period: June 22, 2024 to March 11, 2025 (Greenland National Election)
  • Embedding regression following Rodriguez et al. (2023)
  • Hand-coded training data for supervised context description models
  • Frame dimensions: Sovereignty, Realm, Security, Historical, Democratic, Trump

Timeline of Trump's Greenland Statements

  • August 2019: Initial leak to Wall Street Journal about purchasing Greenland
  • October 2024: Alexander Gray publishes "It's Time for a U.S.-Greenland Free Association Agreement"
  • December 22, 2024: Trump declares on Truth Social that "ownership and control of Greenland is an absolute necessity"
  • January 7, 2025: Trump Jr. visits Nuuk for 4 hours
  • January 15, 2025: Mette Frederiksen has a 45-minute "awful" phone call with Trump
