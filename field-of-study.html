Beyond Attainment: How Field of Study Shapes the Political Returns to Higher Education

Status: Working Paper Methods: Heterogeneous Treatment Effects / Propensity Score Methods

Abstract

This paper asks whether it is what you study — not just whether you study — that shapes the political returns to higher education. Comparing two European countries with contrasting educational systems, we decompose tertiary education effects by field of study and examine what this reveals about the mechanisms behind education's political consequences.

Full abstract available upon request.

Key Contributions

  • Cross-national comparison of field of study effects on political outcomes
  • Heterogeneous treatment effects approach to educational stratification
  • Insights into socialization versus selection mechanisms in higher education
